Habibi's Travel Guide

A weekend for a habibi, his lady, and the little one.
StayWestin Lake Las Vegas
DatesApr 20 – 21, 2026

— Kelmten min albi · A few words from the heart —

Habibi. Lake Las Vegas is the Vegas the tourists don't know about — a Mediterranean-style resort on a private 320-acre lake, twenty-five minutes from the Strip but a world apart. Cobblestone piazzas, gondolas on the water, a sandy beach, no casino carpet underfoot. Feels a little like the coast back home.

Which is the right call for this trip. You get the romance. You get the wow. You skip the neon hangover. And the little one gets a beach.

What's below is not everything Vegas has — only what actually fits this weekend, for this party, in this order.

Sahtein · صحتين · to your health

Fine Dining

For the one night grandmother or a sitter takes the little one.
Marssa Steak & Sushi $$$
Inside The Westin · 0 min walk · AAA Four-Diamond
Aged wagyu, Japanese whisky list, Asian-fusion sushi. The move is staying on property the first night — walk from the room, order the tasting progression, be back before the toddler stirs. Four-diamond food without a car.
Comos Steakhouse $$$
MonteLago Village · 8 min walk along the lake
Signature steaks and seafood at the foot of the Spanish Steps. Great wine list, warm room, white tablecloths. The walk back along the water at night is the whole experience.
Le Cirque at Bellagio $$$$
Las Vegas Strip · 25 min drive · Forbes Five-Star
AAA Five-Diamond French — Adam Tihany's silk-tent room, white-glove service. This is the special-occasion dinner of the trip. Ask for a table facing the Bellagio fountains; the show goes off every fifteen minutes while you eat.
Top of the World $$$
The STRAT · 30 min drive · 800 ft above the Strip
The room rotates a full 360 in eighty minutes — you see the whole valley while you eat. Old-school Vegas romantic. Not the best food of the trip, but the most memorable view.

Local Favorites

Casual, charming, easy with the baby in tow.
Medici Café & Terrace $$
Hilton Lake Las Vegas (next door) · 5 min walk
The best breakfast in the entire area. A stunning Tuscan courtyard with a fountain, lake views, strollers welcome. Book the first morning — eggs benedict, fresh juice, the little one has room to wander. This alone sets the tone of the weekend.
Luna Rossa Ristorante $$
MonteLago Village · 8 min walk
Italian on a patio directly over the water. Kid-friendly pasta, great Chianti list, romantic lighting as the sun drops behind the lake. This is the early dinner — six sharp, before the little one hits the wall.
Sonrisa Grill $$
MonteLago Village · 8 min walk
Gourmet Mexican and a serious tequila bar. Lively but not loud, patio seats, guacamole made tableside. Good lunch after a morning on the beach or at the chocolate factory.
Mrs. Coco Café $
The Village at Lake Las Vegas · 10 min walk · 20 Via Bel Canto
A glamorous hidden-gem café tucked into the village — specialty coffees, pastries, a chandelier bigger than the room. The move for a mid-afternoon coffee while your lady browses the shops.
Azzurra Cucina Italiana $$
Water Street, Downtown Henderson · 15 min drive
The local favorite the tourists miss. Owned by a Henderson architect, run by Chef Alessandra Maderia — handmade pastas, thin-crust pizzas, deep local wine list. Worth the drive for a quieter neighborhood feel.

Enta wa hiyye · إنت وهي · just you and her

Just the Two of You

For nap time, or the evening once she's down.
Gondola Ride on the Lake $$
MonteLago Village dock · 8 min walk · ~30 min ride
A real gondolier, a real Italian song, sunset over the water. Book the last slot before sunset — gold hour on the lake is the most romantic moment of the weekend. Take the little one if you want (she'll be hypnotized) or leave her with grandma and make it champagne only.
Spa Moulay at the Westin $$$
On property · 0 min walk
Couples massage room — two tables, candles, eucalyptus steam. Book the ninety-minute "couples escape" during the toddler's afternoon nap. Grandmother watches, you get a spa date, no one misses anything.
Bellagio Fountains & Conservatory Free
Las Vegas Strip · 25 min drive
The dancing fountains are still the most romantic free thing in Las Vegas — every fifteen minutes after dusk. Walk the Conservatory first (a full botanical garden inside the lobby), then stand at the rail outside for the 9 p.m. show. Hold her hand during the Sinatra number.
Red Rock Canyon at Sunset $20/car
West of the Strip · 35 min drive
A 13-mile scenic loop through red sandstone cliffs — the desert lights up orange in the last hour of daylight. Stay in the car or stop at two of the overlooks. Bring a blanket for the back seat; the little one usually sleeps on the drive home.
High Roller Observation Wheel $$
The LINQ, Strip · 25 min drive · 30 min ride
The largest observation wheel in North America, 550 feet up. Private champagne cabin option if you want the full romance play. Do it at night when the Strip is lit.
— IV —
Ma'al sghireh · مع الصغيرة · with the little one

With the Little One

Toddler-tested, stroller-friendly, nap-window-safe.
Westin Lake Beach Free for guests
On the resort · 0 min walk
A real sand beach with shallow-entry lake water, right off the hotel. This alone justifies the whole trip. Go mid-morning before the sun climbs — hat on her, toes in the water, a bucket from the concierge. She'll remember this for years without knowing why.
Lion Habitat Ranch $$
Henderson · 20 min drive
She can feed a cracker to a real giraffe named Ozzie. They also have a troop of lions behind safe glass. Small, shaded, easy stroller paths, in-and-out in an hour. Toddler core memory unlocked.
Ethel M Chocolate Factory + Cactus Garden Free
Henderson · 15 min drive
Self-guided factory tour (fifteen minutes), free chocolate sample at the end, then a gorgeous 3-acre cactus garden out back. Stroller-friendly paths, shade, benches. A perfect after-nap stop before dinner.
DISCOVERY Children's Museum $$
Downtown Las Vegas · 25 min drive
58,000 square feet, and inside there's a zone called Toddler Town specifically for 2-year-olds — soft-crawl spaces, mining buckets, train engineer play, animal sound stations. If the weather turns or the Strip is too much, this is the rainy-day answer.
Springs Preserve $$
West Las Vegas · 25 min drive · 180 acres
Botanical gardens, a butterfly habitat, a small desert zoo, playgrounds. A genuine break from the neon. Pack a picnic; there are shaded lawns the little one can run on while the adults sit.
Bellagio Conservatory Free
Las Vegas Strip · 25 min drive
A 14,000-square-foot indoor botanical installation in the Bellagio lobby, changed seasonally. The spring display should still be up in April. Toddlers lose their minds over the giant flower sculptures. Pair it with the fountains right outside.

Yalla · يلا · let's go

The Vegas Classics

If a Vegas trip feels incomplete without the bucket-list shot.
Hoover Dam Free to view
East of Henderson · 25 min drive
726 feet tall, built in five years during the Depression. You can drive across the top for free, or pay for the power-plant tour. The best photograph is from the Mike O'Callaghan–Pat Tillman Memorial Bridge — a short walk from the parking lot, better angle than the dam itself.
Seven Magic Mountains Free
South of the Strip · 40 min drive
Seven towers of stacked, painted neon boulders rising out of empty desert — a public art installation by Ugo Rondinone. Free, no tickets, just drive up and walk over. The photograph of the trip. Best in the last hour of daylight.
Fremont Street Experience Free
Downtown Las Vegas · 30 min drive
Old-school neon Vegas — a five-block canopy of LEDs doing synchronized music shows over original Glitter Gulch. Street performers, live bands, the SlotZilla zipline if you feel bold. Better after the little one is in bed.
The Neon Museum $$
Downtown Las Vegas · 28 min drive
The graveyard where legendary Vegas signs go to retire — the Stardust, the Sahara, the original Moulin Rouge. Take the night tour; half the signs are restored to working light and they are breathtaking in the dark.

The woman remembers how her child was treated on a trip. Pitch the whole weekend around the little one's comfort — her nap, her bedtime, her sand beach — and you score double with the girlfriend.

The Toddler Rhythm

The pro move most men don't know. Follow this and the whole trip works.
8 – 9 a.m.
Breakfast at Medici Café. Everyone wakes slowly. Coffee is good.
9 – 11 a.m.
Activity window. Beach, giraffe, chocolate factory — before the sun climbs and before her nap.
12 – 2 p.m.
Nap. Back to the room. This is when you book the couples spa or order lunch to the lanai.
3 – 5 p.m.
Second activity — the lighter one. Gondola, cactus garden, a walk through the village.
6:00 p.m.
Dinner — sharp. Not seven, not eight. Push it later and you will spend it holding a screaming toddler instead of holding her hand.
7:30 p.m.
Little one down. Adult time begins — fountains on the Strip, a nightcap at the bar, or the patio with the door cracked.

One Night, Three Beats

Medici for breakfast. Westin beach mid-morning. Gondola at sunset with the little one on her lap. Luna Rossa for a 6 p.m. dinner. Bellagio fountains when she is finally asleep.

Anyone can book a hotel. This is how you make her remember the weekend.
