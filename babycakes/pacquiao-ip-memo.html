Question Presented

What does Pacquiao actually own, and where is the exposure?

What trademarks, trade names, and parallel IP rights does Manny Pacquiao currently hold or control, and where is the material exposure — for licensors, enforcers, or prospective counsel?

— II —
Short Answer

On paper, his US federal coverage is thinner than his footprint warrants.

Pacquiao's Philippine registrations are the backbone of his enforceable IP. In the United States, the practical protection of his name and likeness today rests primarily on California's Right of Publicity statute and common-law trademark rights — not on live USPTO registrations. The flagship federal word mark was abandoned in 2010 and has not visibly been refiled.

— V —
Parallel Rights

Protection that doesn't require federal registration.

i. California Right of Publicity — Cal. Civ. Code § 3344

Unauthorized commercial use of Pacquiao's name, voice, signature, photograph, or likeness in California triggers statutory damages of the greater of $750 or actual damages, plus defendant's profits, plus attorneys' fees and potential punitive damages. California's statute is among the strongest in the country and applies regardless of federal trademark registration. This is the real enforcement tool in California today.

ii. Common-Law Trademark

Continuous commercial use of "Manny Pacquiao," "MP Promotions," and the fist logo in the United States creates common-law trademark rights in territories of use, enforceable under Lanham Act § 43(a). Weaker than a federal registration — no nationwide constructive notice, no presumption of validity — but still a viable cause of action.

iii. Copyright on Image Assets

Photographs, video, and design assets (including the fist logo treated as an artwork) carry separate copyright protection, typically held by photographers or designers or assigned to promotional entities.

— VI —
The Gap Analysis

Why the under-registration matters.

The 2009 abandonment of the MANNY PACQUIAO USPTO mark (serial 77737664) is material. For roughly fifteen years, Pacquiao's most obvious US word mark has had no federal trademark shield. Opening a US promotional arm in Beverly Hills in 2025 without visible concurrent USPTO filings is atypical for a celebrity of this tier.

Three possibilities, in order of likelihood:

  1. Filings are pending but not yet indexed — USPTO publishing typically lags six to twelve months. Most likely.
  2. Filings are held in a separate SPV or licensing entity that we haven't identified.
  3. Pacquiao's US counsel has not yet locked it down — rare at this level, but possible.

— VII —
Strategic Implications

What this means, depending on where you sit.

— i. —

Licensing & co-branding

Counterparty identification is critical. Different Pacquiao assets (boxing promotion vs. apparel vs. gaming vs. foundation) may sit with different entities. Contracting with the wrong licensor yields no valid license.

— ii. —

Enforcement against unauthorized use

California Right of Publicity is the primary weapon today, not federal trademark. Venue California is plaintiff-favorable. Common-law TM adds a Lanham Act § 43(a) layer but is geographically limited to actual territories of use.

— iii. —

IP portfolio rebuild

US word mark refiling, logo registrations, merchandise-class filings, domain audit, social-handle sweeps, entity-chart cleanup. Textbook work after geographic expansion. Celebrities at this tier typically retain dedicated IP counsel for this rebuild.

— iv. —

Advisory around the US rollout

Trademark filings, licensing framework, sponsorship templates, NIL structuring for MP Promotions' stable of fighters. The Beverly Hills launch is less than six months old. Hot moment, not hypothetical future work.
